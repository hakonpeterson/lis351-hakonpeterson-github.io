Exploring Black Holes

A black hole is an astronomical object with a gravitational pull so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape it. Its event horizon defines the boundary where the velocity needed to escape exceeds the speed of light, which is the speed limit of the cosmos.

Formation of Black Holes

Black holes come into existence through various processes:

  • Stellar Black Holes: Most commonly formed from massive stars that collapse under their own gravity after exhausting nuclear fuel.
  • Supernova Explosions: When a massive star reaches the end of its life, a supernova explosion can leave behind a stellar black hole.
  • Supermassive Black Holes: These giants reside at the centers of galaxies and likely form through mergers and accretion of matter.
  • Primordial Black Holes: Hypothetical black holes formed shortly after the Big Bang, their origin remains speculative.
