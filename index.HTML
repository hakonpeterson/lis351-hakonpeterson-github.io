What are Planets?

A planet is a celestial body that orbits around a star, such as our Sun. These cosmic wanderers come in various sizes, from small rocky worlds to massive gas giants. Here are the key points to understand:

  1. Orbiting the Sun: Planets follow elliptical paths around the Sun. Their gravitational pull keeps them in orbit, creating a delicate cosmic dance.

  2. Solid or Gaseous: Some planets, like Earth, have solid surfaces made of rock and metal. Others, like Jupiter, consist mainly of gases like hydrogen and helium.

  3. Clearing Their Orbits: Planets “clear their orbits” by dominating their regions, sweeping away smaller debris. This distinguishes them from smaller objects like asteroids and comets.

  4. Our Solar System: Our solar system boasts eight recognized planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Each has its unique characteristics and mysteries waiting to be explored.
